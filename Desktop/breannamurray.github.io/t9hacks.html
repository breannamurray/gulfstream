A few weeks ago, I made it to my very first hackathon where I just relaxed and enjoyed the experience. I decided not to participate in the hacking competition so that I could get acclimated to this new experience. Ever since, I am addicted to hackathons. I decided to attend T9Hacks at the University of Colorado, Boulder where I would actually work on a project. This time, I wasn't so relaxed.

For 24 hours straight, myself and 3 other hackers would collaborate to work on a project. Our team consisted of a back-end developer, a front-end developer, myself, who is an up and coming front-end developer, and one other teammate who had no prior experience. Overall, our team got along well and it was a great experience

Our project was a website, Roots to STEM, which allowed younger females interested in STEM to find and connect with mentors and find relevant events. We had some challenges which included some issues with our code and connecting to the server. Due to our persistance and the very helpful mentors that were provided, we ended up overcoming these challenges and our project won a prize at the competition!

So how did I contribute? After arriving to the event, we discussed what frameworks we would use. Within a few hours, I was learning Bootstrap which I would use to code the website. The other front-end developer sketched up some website designs and then we turned it into a working prototype overnight. We were doing fairly well on time and by the morning, we were just about done with our project with a few finishing touches left to add.
